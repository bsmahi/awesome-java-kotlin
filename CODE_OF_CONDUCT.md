# 📜 Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, experience level, nationality, gender identity, and expression.

We aim to create a welcoming and inclusive community for learning Java and Kotlin.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being respectful and kind in communication
- Providing constructive feedback
- Respecting differing opinions and experience levels
- Focusing on meaningful discussions related to Java, Kotlin, and JVM ecosystem
- Helping others learn and grow

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment or offensive comments
- Spam or irrelevant promotions
- Disrespectful or toxic language
- Sharing low-quality or misleading resources intentionally

---

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Reviewing contributions fairly
- Maintaining a positive environment
- Removing inappropriate content when necessary
- Clarifying contribution guidelines when needed

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub issues
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Any community interactions related to this project

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening an issue or contacting the maintainers.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

---

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted for the AwesomeJava & Kotlin project to support an open and friendly learning community.
